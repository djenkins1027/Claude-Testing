Role Summary

The CAD/Layout Engineer is responsible for correcting layout drawings based on client-team redlines and preparing work orders for production. Work is currently reviewed by a client contact for design accuracy and a production representative for construction feasibility before release.

This document outlines a four-year pay progression plan alongside the Key Result Areas (KRAs) tied to each step. Growth in scope and independence — not a change in job title — is what supports movement through the plan.

Pay Progression Plan

Year 1 is effective immediately and reflects current tenure and reliability. Years 2 through 4 are tied to the KRA targets detailed later in this document, and will be reviewed together ahead of each effective date.

A second processing computer will be provided to support productive use of system processing time, paired with the Year 2+ expectation under KRA 4 below.

The table below summarizes all four KRAs and the years they are tied to. Year 1 has no additional milestone — it reflects current tenure. Years 2 through 4 build progressively; each stage builds on the last rather than replacing it.

KRA 1 — Drawing Correction Accuracy

Corrects layout drawings based on redlines from the client contact team; all corrected drawings are currently reviewed by the client contact for design accuracy before proceeding.

Year 2 Target — Track recurring redline/correction patterns to identify root causes; maintain or improve accuracy while building a record of correction types and frequency.

Year 3 Target — Demonstrate a measurable reduction in revision rate on tracked, recurring correction types.

Year 4 Target — Earn limited sign-off authority on low-risk, well-defined correction types, reducing the need for full client-contact re-review on routine items.

KRA 2 — Work Order Preparation & Production Handoff

Prepares work orders for production once drawings are approved; all work orders are currently reviewed by the production representative for construction feasibility before release.

Year 2 Target — Maintain consistent accuracy on work order preparation; note recurring feasibility flags raised by the production rep.

Year 3 Target — Demonstrate a measurable reduction in feasibility flags on tracked, recurring order types.

Year 4 Target — Earn limited sign-off on routine, low-risk work orders, reducing the need for full production-rep review on straightforward items.

KRA 3 — Process Documentation & Succession Coverage

Sole engineer in the role; maintains a working checklist for the process (Microvellum-to-work-order process, common correction types, feasibility judgment calls), but it is informal and not yet in a hand-off-ready format.

Year 2 Target — Formalize the existing checklist into a written SOP that is clear and complete enough for another person to follow and use for hand-off.

Year 3 Target — Use the SOP to begin cross-training a backup on core functions.

Year 4 Target — Backup fully cross-trained and able to cover core functions; role no longer single-threaded on one person.

KRA 4 — Time & Resource Management

Attendance and break-time usage, particularly during system processing time, has been identified as an area for improvement.

Year 2 Onward — Full compliance with the updated employee handbook, including attendance and break-time policies. A second processing computer will be provided so that processing time can be used productively rather than requiring idle time.

What Success Looks Like

Year 2

Recurring redline and feasibility-flag patterns are tracked and documented.

The process checklist is formalized into a hand-off-ready written SOP.

Break and attendance practices are fully aligned with the updated handbook, supported by the second processing computer.

Year 3

A measurable reduction in revision rate and feasibility flags is documented against Year 2 baselines.

A backup has begun cross-training using the formal SOP.

Year 4

Limited sign-off authority is in place for low-risk drawing corrections and routine work orders.

A backup is fully cross-trained and able to cover core functions independently.

The role is no longer single-threaded on one person.
